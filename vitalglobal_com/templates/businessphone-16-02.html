Expert Answers to Your VoIP Technical Questions

  • What is VoIP and how does it work?

    VoIP stands for Voice over Internet Protocol. It is a technology that allows you to make voice calls using a broadband Internet connection instead of a regular phone line. VoIP converts your voice into a digital signal that travels over the Internet. If you are calling a regular phone number, the signal is converted to a regular telephone signal before it reaches the destination.

  • What are the requirements to use VoIP?

    To use VoIP, you need a high-speed internet connection, such as DSL or cable, a VoIP phone or a regular phone with an adapter, or a computer with VoIP software. It's also important to have a stable and sufficient internet speed to ensure call quality.

  • Can I use my regular phone for VoIP?

    Yes, you can use your regular phone for VoIP with the help of a VoIP adapter. This adapter connects your analog phone to your VoIP service, allowing you to make and receive VoIP calls.

  • How does VoIP differ from traditional phone service?

    Unlike traditional phone services that use dedicated copper lines, VoIP uses the Internet to transmit calls. This allows for more features, flexibility, and often lower costs. However, it also means that VoIP depends on having a stable internet connection.

  • Can I keep my current phone number when switching to VoIP?

    In most cases, yes. Many VoIP providers offer number portability, which allows you to keep your existing phone number when you switch to their VoIP service.

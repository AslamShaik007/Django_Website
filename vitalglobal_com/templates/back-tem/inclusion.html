Inclusion: Exploring the Concept

Inclusion is a buzzword that has been making headlines in recent years. But what does it mean, and why is it such a hot topic? Inclusion can be defined as the inclusion of people with different abilities, backgrounds, and experiences to create a workplace representative of the population. It is not about making everyone feel comfortable; rather, it is about creating an environment where everyone feels valued and able to contribute their best work.

There are many benefits to including more people in the workplace. It makes employees feel appreciated, but it also leads to better productivity and innovation. Studies have shown that workplaces with more inclusion tend to be more productive and innovative than those that do not. In addition, inclusion can reduce feelings of isolation and discrimination, leading to increased morale and productivity.

While inclusion may seem like a simple concept, there are still many challenges associated with implementing it in the workplace. One of the biggest challenges is determining who qualifies as "included" in the first place. For example, does including someone who uses a wheelchair mean they should be included in all meetings? Or does including someone who is deaf mean they should be able to hear every conversation?
